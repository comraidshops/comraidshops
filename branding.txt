1. THE COMRAID PHILOSOPHY
-------------------------
ComraidShops is a digital cultural ecosystem. It is not just about moving 
inventory; it is about the "Comraid Authority"—the idea that curation is 
the highest form of commerce. 

Comraid is not a singular voice; it is a controlled range of expressions 
where every brand and creator is interpreted through our lens, not imitated. 
We provide the framework that elevates the specific energy of each entity 
within the grid.

The platform functions as a "Terminal" for the modern collector, where 
independent labels are treated with the reverence of a museum exhibition.

2. THE LEXICON (VOICE & TONE)
-----------------------------
Never use secondary or generic terminology. Our language is high-end, 
editorial, and empowering.

- DASHBOARD -> THE COMRAID TERMINAL
  (A command center, not just an account page)
  
- BOOKMARKS/SAVED -> THE ARCHIVES
  (Items are being curated into a permanent collection)
  
- OUTFITS/LOOKS -> FITFRAMES
  (A cinematic view of product synergy)
  
- PRODUCT LIST -> CURRENT ROTATION
  (A fluid, curated selection)
  
- CATEGORIES -> EXHIBITIONS
  (Theme-led collections that tell a single story)

- SIGN OUT -> DISCONNECT / EXIT TERMINAL
  (Reinforcing the "Terminal" experience)

3. VISUAL PILLARS (AESTHETICS)
------------------------------
The "Comraid" look is defined by High-Contrast Digital Luxury.

- COLOR: Forced Dark Mode. 
  #0a0a0a (Background) / #ededed (Foreground). 
  Color should ONLY exist within product photography and editorial images.

- THE GLASS CENTER: Glassmorphism is our primary UI texture. 
  Backdrop Blur (20px-40px). 
  Subtle border glows (white/10%).
  The goal is for the UI to feel like it's floating over the content.

- TYPOGRAPHY:
  * Headers: Cormorant Garamond (Italic, 700wt). 
    Used for a literary, sophisticated "Magazine" feel.
  * System/UI: Geist Sans / Inter.
    Used for technical, high-performance clarity.

4. MOTION DESIGN (THE BREATHING UI)
-----------------------------------
The UI must feel "alive" through micro-interactions.

- STAGGERED ENTRIES: 
  Components should never appear at once. Use 0.05s - 0.1s delays between 
  items to create a "cascading" reveal effect.
  
- SPRING PHYSICS:
  Avoid linear easing. Use spring-based animations (damping: 30, stiffness: 300) 
  to mimic physical weight and momentum.

- BLUR TRANSITIONS:
  Modals (like the Notification slide-over) should use increasing backdrop-blur 
  animations to transition the user's focus from the shop to the terminal.

5. EDITORIAL STANDARDS
----------------------
- IMAGERY: High-fashion, low-distraction. 
- COPY: Avoid sales jargon. No "Buy Now!" or "Sale!". 
- Use: "Curate into Collection" or "Enter Exhibition".
